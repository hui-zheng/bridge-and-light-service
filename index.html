Bridge and Light

Chinese localization for conservative, libertarian and independent content.

Bridge and Light turns English work into publication-grade Chinese for readers on WeChat, Zhihu, and X. We work across conservative, libertarian and independent journalism, cultural and political commentary, philosophy, and theology.

Our brief is not translation. It is 再创作: a faithful, culturally native rewrite that lets a Chinese-diaspora reader meet your work the way an English reader does, at full register, with the references and context they need to follow it.

Beyond the draft

Anyone can paste an English article into ChatGPT now and get a Chinese draft in a minute. The draft is not the deliverable. The distance between that draft and a piece you would publish under your name is the actual work: native register a diaspora reader trusts, names and numbers and legal terms verified against the source, Western references bridged without editorializing, and a human editor's judgment on every line.

Closing that distance reliably, piece after piece, takes real work: a system that gets the best out of both AI and human editors, and the editorial discipline to run every piece through it. We built that system. You get the finished work. We do it so you don't have to.

What we do

  • Long-form Chinese adaptations of English source: interviews, essays, articles, video transcripts.
  • Chinese subtitles for documentary, talk, and interview footage.
  • Fact-checking on names, statistics, and legal citations before any piece goes out.
  • Context for unfamiliar Western references, added without editorializing.
  • Platform-shaped delivery: WeChat 公众号, Zhihu, X long-form and threads, or whatever channel you publish on.
  • A per-article verification record we keep on file and disclose on request.

Who we work with

Two shapes of partner, by invitation:

  • Creators: independent writers, essayists, journalists, interviewers, documentary producers, podcasters, and authors whose English work deserves a serious Chinese reading audience.
  • Campaigns and institutions: political, civic, and cultural organizations whose message needs to reach Chinese-diaspora voters, donors, and readers as a native voice would carry it.

Editorial discipline

Every piece is read against the source for fidelity, read for voice and naturalness, and measured against the standard of contemporary Chinese essay writing before it goes out. Names, numbers, and legal citations are verified, not guessed. We publish under our own name, and we do not publish work we cannot stand behind.

The full editorial process is documented on our methodology page.
